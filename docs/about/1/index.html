About me

I'm located in Bristol, England. I have worked as a freelance consultant in the past and am always interested in impactful opportunities. This can be from accessibility audits, to wireframing or coding front-end components.

Depending on project needs, I can:

  • High-level accessibility audits to understand areas for improvement, focused on best-practices, WCAG compliance, and practical next steps
  • User experience content writing
  • Wireframing and interactive prototypes
  • Creating design language and component libraries
  • Developing new and implementing new components improvements and bugfixes (or advise the team on the improvements)
  • Conducting user testing and synthesizing data into actionable opportunities

Previously, I have worked in-house in start-ups and small businesses, where I focused on designing and building intuitive front-ends. I have also worked as a graphic and product designer.

My strength has always been in multidisciplinary roles. I excel at performing audits and reviews, engaging in user testing, writing design system libraries, and getting hands-on with front-end development. And in engineering projects I typically take on a double role as ux designer/front-end developer.

Cognitive Biases and User Experience

Cognitive biases are shortcuts in thinking that affect user behavior. Leverage them by aligning with users' beliefs and using anchor points. Reinforce user decisions and make important information accessible. Include genuine social proof elements. Always be transparent to avoid manipulation.

Maximizing User Experience | Understanding Short-Term Memory in Website Interaction

Short-term memory is like a mental notepad with limited space. It can hold around 7 chunks of information but forgets them within 20 seconds if not rehearsed. To create user-friendly websites, design with these limits in mind. Chunk information, prioritize key data, minimize distractions, use visuals, reveal details progressively, and provide cues for a better user experience.
